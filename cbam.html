Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism

CBAM is coming. Be positioned, not penalised.

The EU's carbon border tariff reshapes the economics of every carbon-intensive export. Equiterra turns that exposure into a strategy.

The basics

What CBAM actually means

CBAM puts a carbon price on imports into the EU — starting with carbon-intensive goods — to match the cost EU producers already pay. Exporters that can measure, reduce and document emissions win; those that can't, pay.

Who it hits

Iron, steel, aluminium, cement, fertilisers, hydrogen and electricity — first, with scope expanding.

What changes

Importers must report embedded emissions and, from 2026, buy CBAM certificates for them.

The opportunity

Accurate MRV and decarbonisation become a competitive moat, not just a cost.

Key dates

The CBAM timeline

2023–25

Transitional phase

Quarterly reporting of embedded emissions — no financial payment yet, but the data foundation is set.

2026

Definitive regime begins

Importers start purchasing CBAM certificates for embedded emissions. Real cost enters the equation.

2027–33

Free-allowance phase-out

EU ETS free allowances wind down as CBAM scales — exposure rises for unprepared exporters.

Beyond

Scope expansion

More sectors and indirect emissions are brought in. Early positioning compounds.
