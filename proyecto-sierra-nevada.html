The project

Restoring one of the planet's most irreplaceable ecosystems.

The Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta is the world's highest coastal mountain range and a biodiversity hotspot — and the ancestral home of the Arhuaco people. Equiterra is structuring an ARR carbon project that restores degraded forest and watersheds while putting indigenous stewardship and benefit-sharing at the center of the financial architecture.

Community at the core

Built with the Arhuaco, not around them

This project is co-designed with Arhuaco authorities. Carbon revenue flows back into the community through transparent benefit-sharing, and traditional ecological knowledge guides what gets restored and how.

  • Free, prior and informed consent
  • Transparent benefit-sharing agreements
  • Ancestral knowledge guiding restoration
  • Long-term governance with local authorities
